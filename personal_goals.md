# Personal Goals: Ojito Project

This document serves as a reminder of *why* you are building this project, keeping your core motivations at the center of your work.

---

## 🎯 The Core Driver
* **Your Joy**: You find extreme satisfaction and creative energy when other people are actively using and interacting with what you build.
* **The Target**: **10 active, highly engaged users**.
  * Keep this target small. We do not need thousands of users to start. Ten people scanned, browsing, and giving you feedback is enough to fuel your momentum and validate the product.

---

## 🛑 The Bottleneck We Are Overcoming
1. **The Old Loop**: Build in isolation ➔ launch online ➔ no distribution/traffic ➔ zero users ➔ zero feedback ➔ loss of interest ➔ abandoned project.
2. **The New Strategy**: Break the loop by bringing the feedback loop as close to day one as possible.
   * We are not selling software online into a void.
   * We are building a high-touch, **local pilot** where you can see the direct results of your code immediately.

---

## 🚶‍♂️ The Action Plan for San Mateo Atenco
* Since you live in San Mateo Atenco (a hub for local shoe manufacturing and commerce), you have direct access to local shops, cafes, and stalls.
* **The "No-Sales" Approach**:
  1. Build a stunning MVP template.
  2. Approach 2 or 3 local business owners (like a shoe store or cafe).
  3. Offer a **free, done-for-you pilot**: You shoot the short videos on your phone, upload them, set up their digital catalog, and print a QR code stand for their counter.
  4. Watch real customers scan the QR codes.
  5. Gather feedback face-to-face and iterate.
